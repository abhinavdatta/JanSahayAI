* Project Name: JanSahayAI
* Problem Statement: Citizens across Bharat miss government benefits because information is complex, scattered, and hard to understand.

2. Target Audience
* Primary Users: Rural citizens, small and marginal farmers, elderly citizens, and daily-wage workers.
* Secondary Users: Students and first-time smartphone users.
* User Constraints: Low literacy levels and limited digital skills.

3. Functional Requirements
3.1. Interaction & Interface
* FR-01 (Voice-First): The solution must prioritize voice interaction, allowing users to "speak, don't type."
* FR-02 (Local Language): Support for regional languages (specifically Telugu and Hindi) for both input and output.
* FR-03 (Text Fallback): Users must have the option to interact via simple text if voice is not preferred.

3.2. Core Features
* FR-04 (Scheme Information): Provide details on government schemes and eligibility criteria.
* FR-05 (Civic Resources): Locate nearest civic facilities (e.g., hospitals, RTO offices).
* FR-06 (Step-by-Step Guidance): Offer guidance on required documents and application processes.
* FR-07 (Market & Weather): Provide access to market data and weather updates for farmers.

4. Non-Functional Requirements
* NFR-01 (Connectivity): Must work well in low-bandwidth environments (2G/Edge).
* NFR-02 (Device Compatibility): Must function on basic smartphones and feature phones.
* NFR-03 (Privacy & Ethics): Ensure user-consented interactions with no misuse or selling of personal data.
* NFR-04 (Accuracy): AI responses must be verified against official sources to avoid misinformation.
* NFR-05 (Scalability): Designed to expand across districts/states and support more languages in the future.

5. Implementation Constraints
* Timeline: Development and deployment timeline is 6 to 8 months.
