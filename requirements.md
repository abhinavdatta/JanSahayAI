<h2>2. Target Audience</h2>
<b>Primary Users:</b> Rural citizens, small and marginal farmers, elderly citizens, and daily-wage workers.<br>
<b>Secondary Users:</b>  Students and first-time smartphone users.<br>
<b>User Constraints:</b> Low literacy levels and limited digital skills.<br>

<h2>3. Functional Requirements</h2>
<h3>3.1. Interaction & Interface</h3>
<br><strong>FR-01 (Voice-First):</strong> The solution must prioritize voice interaction, allowing users to "speak, don't type."
<br><strong>FR-02 (Local Language):</strong> Support for regional languages (specifically Telugu and Hindi) for both input and output.
<br><strong>FR-03 (Text Fallback):</strong> Users must have the option to interact via simple text if voice is not preferred.

<h3>3.2. Core Features</h3>
<br><strong>FR-04 (Scheme Information):</strong> Provide details on government schemes and eligibility criteria.
<br><strong>FR-05 (Civic Resources):</strong> Locate nearest civic facilities (e.g., hospitals, RTO offices).
<br><strong>FR-06 (Step-by-Step Guidance):</strong> Offer guidance on required documents and application processes.
<br><strong>FR-07 (Market & Weather):</strong> Provide access to market data and weather updates for farmers.

<h2>4. Non-Functional Requirements</h2>
 <br><strong>NFR-01 (Connectivity):</strong> Must work well in low-bandwidth environments (2G/Edge).
<br><strong> NFR-02 (Device Compatibility):</strong> Must function on basic smartphones and feature phones.
<br><strong>NFR-03 (Privacy & Ethics):</strong> Ensure user-consented interactions with no misuse or selling of personal data.
<br><strong>NFR-04 (Accuracy):</strong> AI responses must be verified against official sources to avoid misinformation.
<br><strong>NFR-05 (Scalability):</strong> Designed to expand across districts/states and support more languages in the future.
